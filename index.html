The Problem

European defence acquisition is broken.

EU procurement law forces open competition, making single-prime contracting nearly impossible. The result: fragmentation, delays, and no one responsible for end-to-end integration.

Multi-year delays

Procurement cycles measured in years while threats evolve in weeks. Traditional acquisition structures collapse under this tempo.

Incompatible architectures

Every MoD has its own architecture, every integrator uses different standards. Products don't work together.

Low SME participation

Small and mid-sized innovators are locked out. No pathway into MoD ecosystems without a prime contractor gatekeeper.

Sovereignty at risk

Foreign lock-in on critical data paths. ISR intelligence cannot rely on foreign primes for sovereign decision-making.

No integration authority

No one actor has responsibility for end-to-end integration. MoDs must manage dozens of contracts themselves.

Duplicated investments

Nations build in parallel. No shared architecture, testing infrastructure, or integration processes across borders.

Legal Structure

Cooperative PPP + STAK.

The most robust and legally defensible governance model for multi-party capability integration. Public party has influence on governance and ensures sovereignty, the guardians of the mission (STAK-board) assure the direction. Industry participates through the cooperative with economic rights but no dominance.

Coöp

Cooperative

Multiple companies and public actors, no dominance. Adapts to new members and exits. Explicitly accepted for multi-party service providers.

STAK

Trust Foundation

Holds voting shares. Prevents foreign control and dominance. Separates economic rights from governance rights. Ensures continuity and stability.

PPP

Public-Private Partnership

Legitimacy for Art. 346. Direct MoD governance influence without full ownership. Legal justification to contract one entity.

Legal Foundation

Fully compliant. Fully defensible.

The CI answers the core question: how can a MoD legally contract one entity without violating EU procurement law?

Art. 2(1)(c)

Life-cycle Services

Integration, maintenance, upgrades, testing, training, certification, architecture governance, operational support.

Art. 2(1)(d)

Military Purpose

Contracts for specifically military purposes. ISR falls clearly within this scope.

Title III

Structured Subcontracting

MoD contracts the CI. CI subcontracts industry. CI ensures compliance, testing, integration, security and final delivery.

Art. 346 TFEU

Sovereignty Derogation

Essential security interests. Public ownership, neutral governance, architecture protection, data sovereignty requirements.

Arts. 21–23

Security of Information & Supply

Controlled membership and certification solve what open tenders with dozens of random companies cannot.

Meershoek 2022

CSDP Doctrine

Military procurement belongs to military logic, not the internal market. CSDP competences are lex specialis to internal-market law.

Two Engines

What drives the CI.

Every CAYS Capability Integrator runs on two complementary engines. Together they ensure the CI never stands still waiting for a government contract, but continuously builds, integrates, and strengthens the ecosystem.

Engine 1

Government Contracts

Governmental organisations contract the CI through a layered model designed for long-term sovereign capability delivery.

Layer 1

Master Enterprise Contract (MEC)

Long-term sovereign agreement defining outcomes, sovereignty zones, security model, architecture obligations, test gates, and sustainment model.

Government ↔ CI
Layer 2

Annual Tasking Orders (TOs)

Funded work programme per period. Integration, development, upgrades, certification, lifecycle support, new capability modules.

Government ↔ CI
Layer 3

Work Orders / Call-Offs / Capability Blocks

Competitive allocation, interface compliance obligations, test windows, acceptance criteria, exit rights.

CI ↔ Ecosystem Suppliers
Engine 2

CAYS Capability Fund

Private investors join the CI ecosystem through the Capability Fund, ensuring continuous momentum independent of government contract cycles.

Ecosystem Consolidation

Fund and stimulate strategic consolidation between ecosystem actors. Strengthen the industrial base through targeted M&A, joint ventures, and supply chain integration.

New Product Creation

Finance the development of new capability modules, dual-use technologies, and innovation sprints. Turn promising prototypes into certified, deployable products.

Contract Pre-financing

Pre-finance anticipated governmental contracts, enabling the CI and its ecosystem to begin preparation, development, and scaling before formal contract award.

Scalability

Built for multi-governmental participation.

The CI structure is designed from the ground up to accommodate multiple governmental organisations, not only Ministries of Defence, but any public authority with a stake in sovereign capability development. Each participant maintains national sovereignty and control over its own mission data, while sharing architecture, standards, testing infrastructure, and integration processes across borders. The cooperative PPP + STAK model ensures no single nation or organisation can dominate.

IP & Data Governance

Protected by design.

Background IP

Each company retains ownership. CI may use under license only. No forced transfer. Registered in the CI IP Registry.

Foreground IP (CI)

Joint ownership proportional to contribution. CI holds custodial rights. MoDs receive sovereign use rights for defence.

Foreground IP (Member)

IP ownership remains with the ecosystem member who generated it.

Data Sovereignty

Data belongs to MoDs. CI processes as a service. Stored in sovereign infrastructure. Classified data never leaves national domains.

The Problem

European defence acquisition
is structurally broken.

EU procurement law, fragmented industry, and slow acquisition cycles are creating a widening gap between the capabilities Europe needs and the capabilities it can actually deliver.

Procurement takes years

Threats evolve in weeks. Procurement cycles run in years. By the time a system is delivered, the mission has changed.

No integration authority

Nobody is responsible for end-to-end integration. MoDs manage dozens of contracts themselves — and carry all the risk.

Sovereignty at risk

Critical ISR data paths run through foreign-owned primes. Intelligence decisions depend on architectures Europe does not control.

SMEs are locked out

Europe's best defence innovators — small, fast, sovereign — cannot get inside the system without a prime contractor as gatekeeper.

Duplicated spending

Nations build the same capabilities in parallel. No shared architecture, no shared testing, no shared standards — just duplicated costs.

Incompatible systems

Every programme uses different architectures, interfaces, and standards. Products from different vendors simply do not work together.

The Solution

One neutral entity.
Full integration. Full sovereignty.

A CAYS Capability Integrator (CI) is a neutral, publicly anchored, industry-empowered organisation built for a specific capability domain. It is the single point of responsibility for end-to-end integration, architecture governance, testing, certification, and lifecycle services — contracted by governments, operated continuously, built with the best of European industry.

One contract for governments

Sign once. The CI handles integration, subcontracting, testing, certification, and lifecycle. Your programme office stops managing vendors — and starts managing outcomes.

Neutral for industry

No single company dominates. Airbus and a 10-person AI startup compete on equal terms, against the same architecture, through the same open process.

Sovereign by design

Mission data, reference architecture, and integration authority remain under public control. Foreign lock-in is structurally impossible.

Legally defensible

Fully compliant with EU Directive 2009/81/EC, Article 346 TFEU, and national procurement law. No legal shortcuts — just a smarter structure.

Continuously evolving

Capability development runs on a continuous cadence — quarterly strategy, 10-week delivery increments, fortnightly architecture governance. No start-stop procurement cycles.

How It Works

Simple for governments.
Powerful underneath.

Three layers. One operating model. Applied to any capability domain.

01

Government signs the Master Contract

A long-term sovereign agreement with the CI cooperative. One counterparty. Clear outcomes. Sovereignty clauses, architecture obligations, and test gates locked in upfront.

02

The CI integrates the ecosystem

Using the CAYS Reference Architecture, the CI runs open mini-competitions among certified industry partners — primes, SMEs, and startups — and integrates, tests, and certifies every capability block.

03

Capability delivered continuously

Annual tasking orders keep the programme moving. Every 10–12 weeks a new capability increment ships. Governments receive proven capability — not promises — on a repeating cadence.

Governance

Cooperative PPP + STAK.

The most robust and legally defensible governance model for multi-party capability integration. Public party has influence on governance and ensures sovereignty. The guardians of the mission (STAK-board) assure the direction. Industry participates through the cooperative with economic rights but no dominance.

Coöp

Cooperative

Multiple companies and public actors, no single dominant party. Flexible for new members and exits. Explicitly accepted for multi-party service providers under EU law.

STAK

Trust Foundation

Holds voting shares. Prevents foreign control and industrial dominance. Separates economic rights from governance rights. Ensures mission continuity and long-term stability.

PPP

Public-Private Partnership

Provides legitimacy for Article 346 TFEU. Gives MoDs direct governance influence without full ownership. Creates the legal basis for contracting one sovereign entity.
